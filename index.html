Project SEKAI COLORFUL STAGE! feat. Hatsune Miku (also known as "Project SEKAI"or "Proseka") is a rhythm game developed as a collaboration between SEGA, Craft Egg, Colorful Palette, and Crypton Future Media. The game was first released in Japan on September 30, 2020 for iOS and Android devices.

In addition to the original Japanese server, there are four localised versions of the game released internationally. These include the Taiwanese (Traditional Chinese) server, which released on September 30, 2021; the Global (English) server, which released on December 7, 2021; and the Korean server, which released on May 20, 2022. There is also a Simplified Chinese version of the game set to release in Mainland China on March 27th, 2025.

History

Initial Release

On September 5, 2020, the Project SEKAI Colorful Stage! Rehearsal Edit was released; it showcased the rhythm game itself, along with the Virtual Live room, where players were able to interact with one another. The Rehearsal Edit featured three songs; Hibana -Reloaded-, Sweet Magic, and Tell Your World, each having all five levels of beatmap difficulty. The demo ended on September 11th, 2020, and has not been playable since then. The game was released on September 30, 2020. Pre-registration was originally set to begin in late April but was delayed until further notice due to the COVID-19 pandemic. In the fifth Project SEKAI livestream, it was announced pre-registration will start on July 22. Because various preregistration milestones were reached, there would be rewards for everyone who downloaded the game from then on.

International Release

Prior to the Japanese release of Project SEKAI, a statement for "fans outside of Japan" was made on the game's Twitter account, confirming that there were no plans for a simultaneous worldwide release of the game. However, "overseas versions" of the game were teased, and international fans were told to "stay tuned". These "overseas versions" would later result in the release four different localised versions of Project SEKAI, making the game accessible to international players in Chinese, English and Korean.

Localisation and Worldwide Releases

Taiwanese version

世界計畫 繽紛舞台！feat. 初音未來 (Pinyin: Shìjiè Jìhuà Bīnfēn Wǔtái feat. Chūyīn Wèilái, English: SEKAI Project Colorful Stage! feat. Hatsune Miku), is the Taiwanese version of Project SEKAI, published by Nuverse (Hong Kong) Limited. It is often called the "Taiwanese Server" or simply "TW", and is officially referred to as the "Asian Edition". The Taiwanese server is localised into Traditional Chinese, and is available only in Taiwan, Hong Kong and Macau.

Pre-release

The official site, Facebook, and Instagram accounts were all made available on August 31, 2021. On the same day, a closed beta for the game was made available for a limited number of Android users, which lasted from August 31 until September 6th, 2021.

Release

The full game released on September 30th, 2021, exactly 1 year after the Japanese server. On the day of launch, it was available for both iOS and Android, and already ran version 1.7 of the game. Subsequent releases of the "Asian Edition" in Mainland China, South Korea, South-East Asia were also planned, however these plans seem to have been scrapped.

Global Server

HATSUNE MIKU: COLORFUL STAGE! is the English version of Project SEKAI. It is often called the "English Server" or simply "EN", and is officially referred to as the "Global Server". It initally released in various regions across the Americas, Europe, and Oceania. Later, it would also be released South-East Asia.

Pre-release

The official site, Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter accounts were all made available on August 31, 2021. On September 1, 2021, the English version of Project SEKAI was announced, with plans to launch later that year. SEGA announced that they aimed to manage the game in "approximately 130 countries/regions", beginning with the United States of America and Europe[1]. An open beta test began on October 4, 2021 and ended on October 19 of the same year. The following week, the full release date was confirmed to be December 7, 2021.

Initial Release

The full game first launched on the previously confirmed release date of December 7, 2021, for both iOS and Android devices.

Exclusive Content

Several exclusive songs would be added to the English server, many of which making their way to other server

"Revival my dream" cancellation

On September 23, 2022, it was announced that the "Revival my dream" event would be skipped on the English server, due to the inclusion of "expressions deemed inappropriate in general for the game". This included it's associated story, gacha, and cards. Though compensation for the event's cancellation were prepared in the form of in-game gifts and additional content, this decision proved controversial among players. On September 30, 2022, it would be clarified that the commissioned song associated with the event would still be available, and that there were no plans to cancel any future content from the Japanese server.

South-East Asian Release

While South-East Asia was initially planned to receive the Taiwanese "Asian Edition" of the game, official social media accounts announced on September 23, 2023 that South-East Asia would be receiving the global server on September 25, 2023.

Korean Version

Launched under the name "프로젝트 세카이 컬러풀 스테이지! feat.하츠네 미쿠".

Release

On March 8, 2022, pre-registration for the Korean server of the game was released. A beta version was open from April 7 to April 14, 2022. The full version was released on May 20, 2022.

Cut/Removed Songs

As with the release of the Project DIVA series, the Korean version would not receive Senbonzakura.

Chinese Version

On September 30, 2024, pre-registration for the Chinese server opened and was available for players in mainland China. A gacha-style event was held to commemorate the occasion, with possible prizes being a Playstation 5, Nintendo Switch, Hatsune Miku merchandise, or Project SEKAI merchandise. From December 6 to December 20, the server was open for beta testing on Android devices. It is expected to release on March 27, 2025.

Story

Ichika looking up at Miku.

The game is set in Shibuya, Tokyo. A girl named Hoshino Ichika walks through the streets and sees an LCD screen displaying Hatsune Miku.

Upon returning home, she discovers a song named "Untitled" on her smartphone. Out of curiosity, Ichika plays the song, only to be surrounded by a white light and transported to an unknown place. Miku stands in front of Ichika, welcoming her to SEKAI and inviting her to sing together.

Characters

VIRTUAL SINGER

The game features Crypton's Piapro characters: Hatsune Miku, Kagamine Rin, Kagamine Len, Megurine Luka, KAITO, and MEIKO. These characters take the form of VIRTUAL SINGERS (also known as VOCALOIDS).

Groups

The game features 20 new characters that are split into 5 units: Leo/need, MORE MORE JUMP!, Vivid BAD SQUAD, Wonderlands x Showtime, and 25-ji, Nightcord de. Each of the units has one or more VIRTUAL SINGERS advising them.

System

Real World and SEKAI

Players can switch between locations in the real world and SEKAI. You can travel between the two worlds using "Untitled" songs.

Conversations are voiced and animated using Live2D.

New Rhythm Game System

Players have to tap, slide, flick, and trace to match new free-form rhythm notes. Colorful Palette wanted to create a game everyone could play, regardless of skill.

The game features many VOCALOID songs. Players can switch between the original VOCALOID versions and SEKAI versions, sung by the game's characters.

A static background containing the cover of the song is available on all songs, but some songs may have a 2DMV, a 3DMV, or an original MV that can be switched by the player.

A list of currently playable songs can be found here. New songs are being added on a regular basis, with covers of classic VOCALOID songs as well as new songs commissioned for the game.

On February 25, 2020, the first Project SEKAI Song Contest was held, the winning song appearing in the game. Submissions were open until April 30, 2020, and the results of the contest were revealed at the first Project SEKAI livestream.

The winner of the first song contest was Ichinose Lupo, with the song alive, sung by Kagamine Rin. Song contests are still being held, and there are currently more than 40 contest winning songs playable in-game.

Costumes

There are a variety of wearable costumes available in Project SEKAI, along with color variations. A full list of costumes can be found here. Costumes can be obtained through purchasing a Premium Mission Pass or by pulling a 4✰ card from the gacha.

In November 2019, the first costume design contest was held. Two designs, one for girls and one for boys. are chosen in each contest to appear in-game. The winners of the first costume contest were Hibi Hana with Cheerful Chick Dress and Ei-Flow with Starlight.

Costume design contests are held every month. These costumes can be purchased using paid crystals or real-life currency.

Gameplay

Project SEKAI includes multiple types of lives, the main two being Solo Lives and Multi Lives. To participate in a live, you will need to build a team using five cards of any of the 26 playable characters. A player's team cannot contain more than one card of a certain playable character except during Challenge Lives. There are four note types in game: flick notes, tap notes, hold notes, and trace notes.

Solo Live

In a Solo Live, you can choose any song along with the vocals for that song, whether if it's the VIRTUAL SINGER, SEKAI, or Another Vocal (also called "Cover") version.

Multi Live

In a Multi Live (also called "Co-Op Live"), there are two rooms available: the General Room, available to everyone, and the Pro Room, available to players with a talent of 150,000 or more. Pro Room lives receive more rewards than General Room ones. You can also make a private room and invite your friends to play with you, making the room private or public. The gameplay is the same as a Solo Live, except that the song is chosen randomly from the selections of all five players. You cannot replay or pause during a Multi Live.

Cheerful Live

Cheerful Live is a live type only available during Cheerful Carnival events. In this live, you compete against another team in a Multi Live, earning team points if your team gains a higher score than the opposing team. Like in Multi Lives, there are two rooms, General and Pro. Instead of each player choosing a song, and then one song being randomly picked from the pool of chosen songs, a random song from the game is chosen to play. You cannot replay or pause during a Cheerful Live.

Rank Match

In Rank Match, skilled players compete directly against another user to climb the ranks and receive rewards at the end of a season. There are currently only three seasons of Rank Match, as well as two Preseasons.

Live Boosts

Live Boosts are items that boost the rewards, EXP, and event points you obtain after a live. You can use up to 10 boosts per live during an event, and five outside of an event period.

Difficulties

There are six levels of difficulty:

  • Easy (5 - 9)
  • Normal (10 - 15)
  • Hard (15 - 24)
  • Expert (21 - 31)
  • Master (25 - 37)
  • Append (APD23 - APD38)

The Easy, Normal, Hard, Expert and Append difficulties are unlocked by default upon obtaining a song. To unlock a song's Master difficulty, it must be completed on Expert difficulty with not more than seven GOODs, BADs, and MISSes altogether (e.g. 4 GOODs, 1 BAD, 2 MISSes, adding up to 7).

Originally, the NORMAL difficulty had a range of 10 - 14, but since Hatsune Miku no Shoushitsu's NORMAL and HARD difficulty was rerated during the first anniversary, it increased the range to 10 - 15.

HARD difficulty originally had a range of 15 - 20, but for the same reasons as above, it was increased to 15 - 21. Later on, v2.0.0 would increase the difficulty expansion, adding levels 22 and 23. The release of Jouou would further expand the difficulty range to 15 - 24.

EXPERT difficulty originally had a range of 22 - 29. MACHINEGUN POEM DOLL's release would expand that range a bit more, introducing EXPERT level 30. Later on, v2.0.0's rerates would further expand that to 22 - 31. Then, Order Made would be the first level to have EXPERT level 21, which expands the range to what we have today.

MASTER difficulty originally had a range of 26 - 33. With the Gekichumai collaboration songs being added, level 34 was first introduced to that range. A few months later, v2.0.0 rerates would further expand the MASTER difficulty, introducing levels 35 and 36. After that, the first ULTIMATE song released (being What's up? Pop!) with a MASTER level of 37, which introduced multi-finger playing. Later, 25-ji no Jounetsu would release with a level of 25, which expands the range to what we have today.

On September 30, 2023, Project SEKAI's third anniversary, Append was introduced as the sixth difficulty, with a range of 23 - 38, which features charts meant to be played with more than three fingers. The difficulty numbering of Append charts is different from the other five difficulties, so an APD prefix is used in-game. Currently, there are no charts with a difficulty level of APD23 or APD38.

Initially, 13 songs were selected to be added with an Append chart. Since the release of this difficulty level, at least one to three currently in-game songs have been selected to be added with an Append chart each month.

Virtual Live

A Virtual Live is a digital live concert using 3D models, similar to Augmented Reality (AR) or a Vocaloid Concert.

Watch your favorite groups perform with fellow players from all around the world. Encourage the performers with your penlight and items.

Before a live starts, you can talk with other players in the lobby using chat and emotes. The lobby contains the Virtual Shop where you can buy avatar costumes, penlights, and actions. Avatar changes must be done before entering the live room.

15 minutes before a live begins, the live room will open. The live will start at the hour displayed on the ticket in the Virtual Live menu. In the live room, you can send items, use emotes, change the color of your penlight, and perform actions. When the live has finished, you can leave and claim rewards from attending the live.
